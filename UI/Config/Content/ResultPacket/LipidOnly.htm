Quality Check:
Cholesterol
High levels of cholesterol and various blood fats increase your risk for cardiovascular disease.
Total Cholesterol
Normal levels should be less than 200 mg/dL unless accompained by a high HDL value.
HDL (Good) Cholesterol
Helps keep cholesterol from build-up and blockage in the arteries.
Normal leveles are 40 mg/dL or greater for men, 50 mg/dL or greater for women.
LDL (bad) Cholesterol
The main sourcr of cholesterol build-up and blockage in the arteries.
Optimal levels should be less than 100 mg/dL.
Triglycerides
Another form of fat in your blood. Normal levels are less than 150 mg/dL.
Glucose
Glucose is a form of sugar that is the body's primary fuel; glucose is broken down from food into energy or stored. Abnormal low or high levels often indicate metabolic disturbances (e.g. diabetes). Normal levels should be under 100 mg/dL.
Blood Pressure
Blood pressure is recorded as two numbers. Systolic measures the pressure in the arteris when the heart beats. Diastolic measures the pressure between heartbeats. Normal systolic blood pressure is less than 120 mm Hg. Normal Diastolic blood pressure is less than 80 mm Hg. Unusally low levels should be evaluated for clinical significance.
