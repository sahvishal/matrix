Quality Check:

Our Cardiochek machines have a high and low threshold limit for each measured lipid component. If your blood results fall out of one of these threshold ranges, we will not have an absolute number value for that component.

In order to have a KYN report processed for you, we will use an absolute value for that lipid component. A KYN report cannot be processed without absolute values. This practice does not affect your risk factors, modifiable risks, or disease probability calculations. In fact, our machine threshold limits are the same as the plateau limits for calculating risk for your KYN report.
  • If your Total cholesterol result was measured as (>) greater than 400 or (<) less than 100, then the absolute value we will input into your kyn report will be 401 or 99 respectively. your ldl result will also be calculated using this absolute value.
  • If your HDL result was measured as (>) greater than 100 or (<) less than 15, then the absolute value we will input into your kyn report will be 101 or 14 respectively. your ldl result will also be calculated using this absolute value.
  • If your Triglyceride result was measured as (>) greater than 500 or (<) less than 50, then the absolute value we will input into your kyn report will be 501 or 49 respectively. your ldl result will also be calculated using this absolute value.
  • If your Glucose result was measured as (>) greater than 600 or (<) less than 20, then the absolute value we will input into your kyn report will be 601 or 19 respectively. please discuss with your doctor asap as these are considered critical values.
