LOWER EXTREMITY ARTERIAL DUPLEX (LEAD)
The LEAD ultrasound evaluates the two major arteries in the neck that supply blood to the brain. Studies have shown that those with excess plaque in the arteries are at higher risk for stroke and coronary heart disease. As fatty plaque builds up in these blood vessels, clots have the potential to break off, causing strokes. This ultrasound test is used to identify plaque build-up, and other obstructions that increase the risk for stroke.
